The Capuchino Estate Historical Library

This website is designed as a family archive in the style of an early-2000s state historical portal: textured, formal, slightly bureaucratic, and heavily oriented around the biographical narrative of Ludwig Alexander de Capuchino.

The site follows the movement laid out in the archive text itself: rise through Bourbon administrative culture, Atlantic commissions, the Potosí and Lima experiences, the discovery of coffee and milk preparation, inheritance of the Andalusian estate, and the eventual development of a private maritime enterprise governed by his doctrine of comercio combativo.

Archival Summary

Principal Distinctions and Family Position

Capuchino’s archive presents a family of service nobility rather than ancient landed aristocracy. The father, Barclay de Capuchino, is tied to the Madrid Mint and later to the Order of Montesa, while Ludwig is eventually received into the Order of Calatrava after his second Potosí expedition.

Chronology

1689: Born in Spain.

1710–1711: Enters French administrative orbit.

1718: Sails on the San Cristóforo.

1723–1724: Returns to Potosí and expands coffee interests.

1729: Establishes Cádiz office and private venture.

1731–1733: Company system extends across Lima, Havana, and Cádiz.

Research Note

This site works best when treated not as a modern biography but as a preserved institutional archive: formal in tone, documentary in structure, and conscious of both family memory and state record.
