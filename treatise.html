Estrategias fiscales del comercio combativo

This treatise is the intellectual spine of the entire site. It emerges as Capuchino reassesses the weakness of his inherited estate, the limits of bullion dependence, and the inefficiencies created by the rigid structures of Bourbon administration.

The work treats trade not as a peaceful exchange governed by fixed channels, but as a contest over access, timing, and control. In this formulation, advantage belongs to those who move quickly, act discreetly, and preserve authority over routes, cargoes, and source regions.

Core Propositions

Mobility

Effective commerce depends on freedom of movement and the ability to bypass slow channels.

Discretion

Administrative rigidity invites circumvention; successful trade often lies in measured opacity.

Speed

Timing and direct action matter more than ceremonious adherence to cumbersome process.

Connection to the Maritime File
